Phishing Emails

Phishing emails are fraudulent messages designed to trick recipients into revealing sensitive information like passwords or financial details. Attackers impersonate legitimate businesses or organizations to create a sense of urgency.

Examples of indicators for phishing emails include:

  • Unfamiliar Sender Addresses: Email addresses that don’t match the company or individual they claim to represent.
  • Suspicious Links or Attachments: Hidden or shortened links that lead to malicious websites.
  • Urgent or Threatening Language: Messages that pressure the recipient to take immediate action.
  • Grammatical Errors: Phishing emails often contain spelling or grammar mistakes.
